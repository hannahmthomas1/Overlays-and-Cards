Time for Change

Have you ever been on a nice walk or hike in the mountains when a well-meaning friend or family member interupts the peaceful silence by deciding to quiz you about local foliage? Well I have. I love plants, but I am not going to remember their names. But don't fear! There is a simple solution! We can make this world a better place by giving plants logical names you don't have to remember.

The Big Idea

This problem does have a solution! That is, to remove official names of trees and practice creating titles that make logical sense! When you lose something, like your keys, the best place to put it next time is the first place you looked. So, better names for trees are the first words we think of when we see it!

For Example:

Pine Tree
Dark Pointy Tree
Oak
Spotted white trunk with round leaves
Redwood
Really Big

Mountain Trees

Savannah Trees

Water Trees

Desert Trees

Why try?

According to globaltrees.org, there are 60,065 tree species. The average adult knows 20,000-35,000 words. Even if we tried, we would be unable to remember them all. Is it really worth trying?

How to create a name:

  1. Look at tree
  2. Write down the first descriptive words that come to your mind
  3. Share your new name creation
  4. Finally, don't expect anyone to remember!

Use #DenameTrees when sharing on social media.
